tutorial
welcome to wordbomb practice
This tool is a practice companion for the game Word Bomb. It gives you a controlled environment to drill prompts, track your weaknesses, and build the word recall speed you need to compete.
Use the sidebar on the left to switch between tabs. Click the ? button at the bottom of the sidebar at any time to return here.
New here? Read through the pages in order — they build on each other. If you already know the basics, use the left panel to jump to the section you need.
how to play
Each round shows you a short prompt — 2 to 5 letters. Type any valid English word that contains that prompt as a substring, then press Enter.
Example: Prompt is str → valid answers include street, strong, string, abstract.
The prompt text turns green if the prompt is itself a valid dictionary word — meaning you can just type it and submit it directly.
As you type, your input is previewed above the text box with the matching portion highlighted in green, so you can confirm the prompt is inside your word in real time.
Shortcuts:
Enter on empty input → auto-solve (reveals a valid word)
^ or 6 → also triggers auto-solve
Use these when genuinely stuck, but try not to rely on them — struggle mode will notice.
about word bomb
Word Bomb is a multiplayer game where all players share the same prompt at once. You have a limited time to type a valid word containing that prompt — if the bomb goes off before you answer, you lose a life.
The key skill is instant word recall. When you see "str", a word needs to come to mind in under two seconds. This trainer builds that muscle memory.
How to use this trainer effectively:
Focus on prompts where you hesitate, auto-solve, or consistently take more than 3–4 seconds. Those are the prompts that will cost you lives in a real match.
The difficulty % shown for each prompt is its solve rate — roughly how often players answer it successfully before time runs out. Lower % means harder.
Use this trainer to memorise reliable "backup words" — short, common words for each prompt. For example, strength covers str, ren, eng, ngt, and ength all at once.
practice modes
Open the modes tab in the sidebar to choose how you want to practice. Clicking a mode card immediately starts it.
Normal — random prompts filtered by your solve rate range (set in config). Best for general warm-ups.
All prompts — sequential — cycles through every prompt in the game, sorted hardest first. Good for a thorough full-coverage session.
Favorites — random — shuffles your starred prompts. Priority prompts (✦) appear 3× more often.
Favorites — sequential — goes through your starred prompts alphabetically, one by one, without repeating until the full list is done.
Priority only — exclusively drills prompts marked ✦. Great for focused cramming before a session.
Struggle mode — tracks your response times and auto-solve usage per prompt, and weights the ones you struggle with more heavily. See the Struggle Mode page for more.
You can also start favorites practice quickly from the favorites tab using the quick practice buttons.
favorites & priority
Two star buttons appear in the top-right of the prompt card during the game.
★ gold star — favorites this prompt. Saved locally and usable across all favorites practice modes.
✦ pink star — marks a favorited prompt as priority. Only visible once a prompt is already favorited. Priority prompts appear in their own section in the Favorites tab, and get 3× weight in random favorites practice.
Use favorites to build a personal list of prompts you need to work on. Use priority for the ones that are actively costing you lives in matches.
Favorites and priority data are saved in your browser. Use the export / import buttons in the Favorites tab to back them up or transfer them to another device.
struggle mode
Struggle mode automatically identifies prompts you find difficult based on two signals: how long you take to answer, and how often you auto-solve it.
These combine into a struggle score for each prompt. Prompts with higher scores appear more often in the rotation; ones you handle quickly and confidently become rarer.
Recommended flow: Run a normal or all-prompts session first to collect data across the full prompt list. Then switch to struggle mode to automatically focus on your weak spots.
The Modes tab shows which prompts are currently weighted highest — these are your struggle zone. As you improve on them, they'll naturally fade out of the rotation.
Struggle data persists across sessions and resets when you clear stats.
lookup tool
The lookup tab has two independent tools.
Prompt lookup — type a prompt (e.g. STR) to see its solve rate and every valid word that contains it (up to 222). Words are sorted by your current solve sort setting from config.
Word → prompts breakdown — type any word (e.g. STRENGTH) to see every prompt from the game's prompt list that appears inside it, sorted hardest first. Each entry shows just the prompt and its solve rate %.
The breakdown tool is powerful for finding "multi-cover" words — single words that serve as backup answers for several hard prompts at once. This is one of the highest-leverage preparation techniques for Word Bomb.
configuration
Open the config tab to adjust game behaviour.
Solve rate filter — sets which prompts appear in normal mode. Min 0 / Max 50 = only hard prompts. Min 60 / Max 100 = warm-up on easier ones. Has no effect on favorites or all-prompts modes.
Solve sort — controls the word order when auto-solve reveals an answer. "Random" shows variety. "Shortest first" shows easy-to-remember words. "Rare letters first" shows uncommon words useful for Word Bomb bonus rounds.
Theme — 8 visual themes available. Your choice is remembered between visits.
tips & tricks
Build one solid backup word per prompt. You don't need ten options — just one word you can always recall under pressure. Reliability beats variety.
Use the breakdown tool on long words. Words like UNCLEANLINESS, STRAIGHTFORWARD, and STRENGTHENING cover huge numbers of prompts. Find these and memorise them.
Speed matters more than variety. A fast ordinary answer beats a slow perfect one in Word Bomb. Practice until your backup words come out in under a second.
Watch your timer. The timer resets each prompt. If you're consistently hitting 4+ seconds, star the prompt and drill it in priority mode.
Don't over-use auto-solve. It's a crutch. Struggle mode will flag prompts where you auto-solve often and keep them in heavy rotation until you can answer them independently.
Use sequential mode for coverage. Before a serious ranked session, run all-prompts sequential at speed to make sure you have at least one answer ready for every possible prompt.
